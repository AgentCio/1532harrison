38% of San Francisco's population consists of single person households while the average rent for a conventional studio apartment has risen to $2,312/month, making housing affordability for this group a very significant problem.

For decades, single adults have informally shared large, old Victorian flats across the City, yet few-if any-new buildings accommodate this time-honored tradition of sharing and saving.

The 1532 Harrison Coliving Project is an innovative new housing typology designed to help address the City's housing crisis by looking toward what already works, and improving on it with technology and conscious design-without relying on government subsidies.

Rethinking Home

Coliving empowers residents to be active creators and participants in the world around them. Similar to coworking, coliving can cultivate collaboration and serendipity amongst residents and the extended community. Coliving houses support sustainable and affordable lifestyles through sharing and the efficient use of resources and space.

The Neighborhood

We feel that Western SoMa is a crazy-quilt of the experiences, places and people that make San Francisco an extraordinary and unique city. [NEEDS EDITS] seeks to use the urban landscape to celebrate public history, accept diversity, and preserve the legacy of its LGBTQ culture and post-World War II industrial working class roots.
